# Requirements Document

## Introduction

The Green Credit Score feature adds a buyer-facing sustainability reward system to the ReLoop circularity platform. Each buyer accumulates green credits through eco-friendly actions — avoiding unnecessary returns, choosing sustainable shipping, participating in the resale marketplace, and returning items that get refurbished or recycled rather than discarded. Credits are expressed as a tiered score (0–1000), displayed on a dedicated profile page and surfaced as contextual nudges throughout the purchase and return flows. The score unlocks discount vouchers and badge tiers (Seedling → Sprout → EcoChampion → Guardian), closing the loop between buyer behaviour and tangible incentive.

The feature is built on top of the existing `DispositionResult.green_credits` field, `circularity_score`, buyer `eco_preference` data in `buyers.json`, and the return-event pipeline already tracked in `return_events.json`.

---

## Glossary

- **Green_Credit_Score (GCS)**: A single integer in the range [0, 1000] that represents a buyer's cumulative eco-friendly behaviour on the platform.
- **Credit_Engine**: The server-side module responsible for calculating and updating a buyer's Green_Credit_Score.
- **Eco_Action**: A discrete buyer behaviour that earns or reduces green credits (e.g., completing a return whose item is refurbished, making a marketplace purchase).
- **Badge_Tier**: One of four progression tiers — Seedling (0–199), Sprout (200–499), EcoChampion (500–799), Guardian (800–1000) — assigned based on a buyer's current GCS.
- **Green_Voucher**: A discount coupon generated by the Credit_Engine and redeemable at checkout when a buyer's GCS crosses a defined milestone threshold.
- **Score_Widget**: The compact UI component (score ring + badge label) embedded in the Nav bar, dashboard, and product pages.
- **GCS_Profile_Page**: A full-page buyer view at `/profile/green-score` showing score breakdown, badge tier, action history, and available vouchers.
- **Action_Log**: The ordered list of Eco_Actions with timestamps, credit deltas, and descriptions stored per buyer.
- **Milestone**: A GCS value (200, 500, 800, 1000) at which a Green_Voucher is automatically issued.
- **Buyer**: A platform user identified by a buyer ID, as defined in `buyers.json` and the existing `Buyer` type in `types/index.ts`.
- **Disposition_Decision**: The output of the AI disposition pipeline (`DispositionResult`), specifically the `decision` field and `green_credits` field.
- **Shipping_Option**: A shipping method selected during checkout, classified as `standard`, `consolidated`, or `carbon_offset`.

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Credit Earning via Eco-Actions

**User Story:** As a buyer, I want to earn green credits for eco-friendly actions, so that I am rewarded for sustainable behaviour on the platform.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a buyer completes a return and the Disposition_Decision is `refurbish` or `resell`, THE Credit_Engine SHALL award the buyer 50 green credits.
2. WHEN a buyer completes a return and the Disposition_Decision is `donate`, THE Credit_Engine SHALL award the buyer 30 green credits.
3. WHEN a buyer completes a return and the Disposition_Decision is `recycle`, THE Credit_Engine SHALL award the buyer 20 green credits.
4. WHEN a buyer completes a return and the Disposition_Decision is `exchange`, THE Credit_Engine SHALL award the buyer 10 green credits.
5. WHEN a buyer purchases an item from the Marketplace, THE Credit_Engine SHALL award the buyer 40 green credits.
6. WHEN a buyer selects `consolidated` shipping at checkout, THE Credit_Engine SHALL award the buyer 15 green credits.
7. WHEN a buyer selects `carbon_offset` shipping at checkout, THE Credit_Engine SHALL award the buyer 25 green credits.
8. WHEN a buyer completes a return whose returned item has a `circularity_score` (on a 0–100 scale) greater than or equal to 70, THE Credit_Engine SHALL award an additional 10 green credits as a high-circularity bonus, cumulative with the disposition award from criteria 1–4.
9. WHEN a buyer initiates a return with `return_reason` equal to `changed_mind` or `wrong_variant` within 72 hours of the delivery timestamp recorded on the order, THE Credit_Engine SHALL deduct 10 green credits from the buyer's GCS, with a minimum resulting GCS of 0.
10. THE Credit_Engine SHALL record every credit award and deduction as an Eco_Action entry in the buyer's Action_Log, including the action type, credit delta (as a signed integer), timestamp in UTC ISO-8601 format, and associated order or product ID.
11. THE Credit_Engine SHALL process each eligible event at most once; resubmitting or retrying the same event identifier SHALL NOT result in a duplicate credit award or deduction.

### Requirement 2: Score Calculation and Badge Tier Assignment

**User Story:** As a buyer, I want my Green_Credit_Score to reflect my cumulative eco-friendly actions and be categorised into a meaningful tier, so that I can understand my environmental impact at a glance.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Credit_Engine SHALL compute a buyer's GCS as the sum of all positive credit awards minus all deductions recorded in the buyer's Action_Log, clamped to the range [0, 1000].
2. WHEN a buyer's GCS is in the range [0, 199], THE Credit_Engine SHALL assign the buyer the Badge_Tier `Seedling`.
3. WHEN a buyer's GCS is in the range [200, 499], THE Credit_Engine SHALL assign the buyer the Badge_Tier `Sprout`.
4. WHEN a buyer's GCS is in the range [500, 799], THE Credit_Engine SHALL assign the buyer the Badge_Tier `EcoChampion`.
5. WHEN a buyer's GCS is in the range [800, 1000], THE Credit_Engine SHALL assign the buyer the Badge_Tier `Guardian`.
6. THE Credit_Engine SHALL recalculate a buyer's GCS and Badge_Tier within 5 seconds of any Eco_Action being recorded for that buyer.
7. FOR ALL buyers, the Badge_Tier assigned SHALL correspond exactly to the tier whose range contains the buyer's current GCS (round-trip property: recomputing from the same Action_Log SHALL produce an identical GCS and Badge_Tier).

---

### Requirement 3: Milestone Voucher Generation

**User Story:** As a buyer, I want to receive a discount voucher when I reach a score milestone, so that my eco-friendly behaviour translates into tangible rewards.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a buyer's GCS crosses a Milestone value (200, 500, 800, or 1000) for the first time, THE Credit_Engine SHALL generate exactly one Green_Voucher for that Milestone.
2. THE Credit_Engine SHALL NOT generate a duplicate Green_Voucher for a Milestone that has already been crossed by the same buyer.
3. THE Green_Voucher for the 200-point Milestone SHALL carry a 5% discount value.
4. THE Green_Voucher for the 500-point Milestone SHALL carry a 10% discount value.
5. THE Green_Voucher for the 800-point Milestone SHALL carry a 15% discount value.
6. THE Green_Voucher for the 1000-point Milestone SHALL carry a 20% discount value.
7. THE Credit_Engine SHALL assign each Green_Voucher a unique voucher code, an expiry date 90 calendar days from the generation date, and a status of `active`.
8. WHEN a Green_Voucher's expiry date is reached, THE Credit_Engine SHALL update the voucher's status to `expired`.
9. IF a buyer attempts to redeem a Green_Voucher whose status is `expired`, THEN THE Credit_Engine SHALL reject the redemption and return an error indicating the voucher has expired.

---

### Requirement 4: GCS Profile Page

**User Story:** As a buyer, I want a dedicated page showing my full green credit history, badge tier, and available vouchers, so that I can track my environmental contribution over time.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE GCS_Profile_Page SHALL display the buyer's current GCS as a numeric value and as a visual progress ring indicating progress toward the next Milestone.
2. THE GCS_Profile_Page SHALL display the buyer's current Badge_Tier label and a description of the criteria for the next Badge_Tier.
3. THE GCS_Profile_Page SHALL display a chronologically ordered Action_Log listing each Eco_Action with its description, credit delta, and timestamp.
4. THE GCS_Profile_Page SHALL display all Green_Vouchers earned by the buyer, showing the voucher code, discount value, expiry date, and status (`active` or `expired`).
5. WHEN the GCS_Profile_Page is loaded, THE GCS_Profile_Page SHALL fetch and display data within 2 seconds under normal network conditions.
6. THE GCS_Profile_Page SHALL be accessible at the route `/profile/green-score`.
7. IF no Eco_Actions have been recorded for a buyer, THEN THE GCS_Profile_Page SHALL display a GCS of 0, the `Seedling` badge, an empty Action_Log, and a prompt encouraging the buyer to take their first eco-friendly action.

---

### Requirement 5: Score Widget in Navigation and Contextual Surfaces

**User Story:** As a buyer, I want to see my Green_Credit_Score in the main navigation and on product pages, so that my score is always visible without navigating to a separate page.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Score_Widget SHALL display the buyer's current GCS integer and Badge_Tier icon in the Nav bar on every page of the application.
2. WHEN a buyer's GCS increases as a result of an Eco_Action, THE Score_Widget SHALL update to reflect the new GCS within 5 seconds without requiring a full page reload.
3. THE Score_Widget SHALL link to the GCS_Profile_Page when clicked.
4. WHERE the platform renders a product page, THE Score_Widget SHALL display the estimated green credits the buyer would earn by purchasing that item from the Marketplace.
5. WHERE the platform renders the return flow result screen, THE Score_Widget SHALL display the green credits awarded for the completed return alongside the Disposition_Decision.

---

### Requirement 6: Return-Flow Nudge for Eco-Unfriendly Returns

**User Story:** As a buyer, I want to be informed of the credit impact before confirming a discretionary return, so that I can make an informed decision about whether the return is necessary.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a buyer selects `changed_mind` or `wrong_variant` as the return reason, THE Return_Flow SHALL display a nudge message stating the number of green credits that will be deducted if the return is confirmed.
2. WHEN a buyer selects `defective`, `wrong_size`, or `not_as_described` as the return reason, THE Return_Flow SHALL NOT display a credit-deduction nudge.
3. THE Return_Flow SHALL proceed to the next step regardless of whether the buyer acknowledges the nudge, without blocking the return.
4. WHEN the return is confirmed after a nudge is displayed, THE Credit_Engine SHALL apply the 10-credit deduction as specified in Requirement 1, Criterion 9.

---

### Requirement 7: API Endpoints for Green Credit Score

**User Story:** As a developer, I want well-defined API routes for reading and updating green credit data, so that all frontend surfaces can consume a consistent data contract.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE GCS_API SHALL expose a `GET /api/green-credit/[buyerId]` endpoint that returns the buyer's current GCS, Badge_Tier, Action_Log, and list of Green_Vouchers.
2. THE GCS_API SHALL expose a `POST /api/green-credit/action` endpoint that accepts a buyer ID, action type, associated entity ID, and optional metadata, and records the Eco_Action and updates the GCS.
3. WHEN a `GET /api/green-credit/[buyerId]` request is made for a buyer ID that does not exist in the platform's buyer data, THE GCS_API SHALL return a 404 status code with a descriptive error message.
4. WHEN a `POST /api/green-credit/action` request is made with a missing or invalid action type, THE GCS_API SHALL return a 400 status code with a descriptive error message.
5. THE GCS_API SHALL return all responses in JSON format with a Content-Type header of `application/json`.
6. FOR ALL valid buyer IDs, a `GET` request followed by a `POST` action followed by another `GET` request SHALL return a GCS in the second `GET` response that equals the GCS from the first `GET` response plus the credit delta of the posted action (round-trip property).

---

### Requirement 8: Dashboard Integration

**User Story:** As an operations manager, I want to see aggregate green credit statistics on the Ops Dashboard, so that I can monitor the programme's engagement and environmental impact.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Dashboard SHALL display the total number of Green_Vouchers issued across all buyers.
2. THE Dashboard SHALL display the aggregate GCS earned across all buyers for the current calendar month.
3. THE Dashboard SHALL display the count of buyers who have reached each Badge_Tier (Seedling, Sprout, EcoChampion, Guardian).
4. WHEN the Dashboard data is fetched, THE Dashboard SHALL include the existing `green_credits_awarded` KPI already present in the dashboard API response.
5. THE Dashboard SHALL update the green credit statistics on every page load without requiring a separate manual refresh action.
